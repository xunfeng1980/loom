<objective>
Replace the kloom harness's hard-error-only behavior with a typed four-state outcome that distinguishes "compared and matched", "compared and diverged" (hard fail), "referee absent" (skip), and "program unsupported for K oracle" (skip-with-reason). Add the LOOM_ALLOW_K_ORACLE_SKIP env convention, a krun timeout, garbled-output detection, and an unsupported-construct predicate that refuses to serialize placeholder-lowered programs (Min/Max/Bytes).

Purpose: The placeholder holes (Min/Max → `0`, Bytes → `0`) and silent empty-trace-on-garbled-output poison the very comparison this phase hardens. krun-absence currently fail-closes the production native route instead of skipping. This plan makes the K oracle's outcome honest and typed so plan 48-02 can act on divergence and plan 48-03 can wire the skip convention into gates.

Output: `KOracleOutcome` enum, an `unsupported_for_k_oracle` predicate, env-gated skip + timeout + garbled detection in `run_kloom`, the typed outcome threaded through `reference_model_trace_for_batch`, and a new skip-semantics test file.
</objective>
